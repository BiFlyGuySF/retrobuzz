17 One-Hit Wonders from the ’90s You Still Know by Heart

RetroBuzz Team2025-08-27
Ad slot (336×280)

Admit it—you’re humming at least three of these already.

  1. “Mambo No. 5” – Lou Bega. An absolute wedding floor magnet.
  2. “Blue (Da Ba Dee)” – Eiffel 65. We are aware of the chorus, thank you.
  3. “Torn” – Natalie Imbruglia. The soft-cry-in-your-bedroom national anthem.
  4. “Tubthumping” – Chumbawamba. You get knocked down, then immediately scream along.
  5. “No Rain” – Blind Melon. Bee girl supremacy.
  6. “Breakfast at Tiffany’s” – Deep Blue Something. “As I recall, I think we both kinda liked it…”
  7. “Bitter Sweet Symphony” – The Verve. Strings for days; drama for years.
  8. “Closing Time” – Semisonic. Every bar’s closing ritual.
  9. “Steal My Sunshine” – Len. Sun-drenched and inexplicably perfect.
  10. “Informer” – Snow. Licky boom-boom down, indeed.
  11. “Baby Got Back” – Sir Mix-a-Lot. Anatomy awareness PSA.
  12. “How Bizarre” – OMC. Self-referential magic.
  13. “I’m Gonna Be (500 Miles)” – The Proclaimers. Shout-sing cardio.
  14. “What Is Love” – Haddaway. Neck braces for the *SNL* head-bobs.
  15. “Bitch” – Meredith Brooks. Catharsis in 3 minutes, 58 seconds.
  16. “Return of the Mack” – Mark Morrison. The comeback we all root for.
  17. “Macarena” – Los del Río. Grandma-approved, universally memed.
